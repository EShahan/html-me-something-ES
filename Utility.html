Utility

If it's between staying alive and utility, you'd better choose staying alive.
However, if staying alive is a done deal, bringing utility can be the difference between your team staying alive, too.
Knowing what types of sets and skills will offer utility to your team can turn the tide!

Flex Spots

Utility sounds great and all, but where am I supposed to put these skills?
Flex Spots are just skills on your bar that aren't overly important.
When you realize you need or want a certain utility skill, these are the first skills you swap out.
Typically the first to go are any skills I didn't recommend in Build Set Up.

Positioning and Misc.

These would be your Chains, Stuns, Roots, Snares, Interrupts, Soft Aggro Tools, and Gap Closers.
Most dangerous enemies in trials are immune to these skills, so they're typically reserved for dungeons or specific trial fights.

Damage Mitigation

"Hey, isn't this the healer's job?"
I mean, sure, if you wanna be anal about it. Realistically though, the healer is pretty busy. Doing what you can to help out goes far.
Now, you aren't a healer, so the skills you pick usually have to be %HP based synergies, minor lifesteal, or damage reduction.

Sustain

Any points a DPS puts into not running out of resources is points they don't put into damage.
Taking skills which offer synergies to restore resources, or that offer regen buffs, will free up room for your DPS to go full glass cannon.
